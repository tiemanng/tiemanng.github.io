excerpt: 'This article re-examines the outcome of the October 1920 Carinthian plebiscite, in which a substantial share of the Slovene-speaking population voted for Austria rather than the Kingdom of Serbs, Croats, and Slovenes. Drawing on disaggregated precinct-level electoral data and newly assembled sociodemographic contextual indicators, it challenges established interpretations that portray the result primarily as an expression of ethnic or linguistic identity, and offers spatial-econometric evidence for a more complex set of economic and institutional determinants of the vote.'
